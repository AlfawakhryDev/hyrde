# Hyrde Link-Building Outreach Kit

**Goal:** land 8–15 quality backlinks (Phase 4) to make the SEO compound faster.
**Your job:** find the contact email, paste a template, personalize line 1, hit send, follow up once.
**Realistic math:** ~30–40 sends → 8–15 links. Most won't reply. That's normal.

Two assets do the heavy lifting:
- **Rate Index** → https://hyrde.net/rates (free data — people link to data)
- **Comparison pages** → https://hyrde.net/compare (for "Upwork alternatives" roundups)

---

## PART 1 — How to build your target list (do this first, ~45 min)

You're looking for pages that *already* link to freelance content, so a link to you is a natural fit. Run these searches in Google and collect URLs into the tracker (Part 3):

**A. "Upwork alternatives" roundups** (highest-value — they literally list competitors)
```
best upwork alternatives 2026
upwork alternatives for clients
top freelance platforms 2026
fiverr alternatives for businesses
toptal alternatives
```
→ For each article on page 1–2, grab the URL + author name. Aim for 12–15 of these.

**B. Freelance-economy / future-of-work blogs** (good fit for the Rate Index)
```
freelance rates 2026
how much to charge freelance developer
freelance economy statistics
freelance pricing guide
```
→ Blogs, newsletters, and resource sites that cite pricing data. Aim for 8–10.

**C. Niche communities (no email needed — you post, don't pitch)**
- Indie Hackers, relevant subreddits (r/freelance, r/forhire — read rules first), Hacker News (Show HN), founder Slack/Discord groups.
- These aren't "links" so much as referral traffic + occasional organic links. Be genuinely helpful, never spammy.

**Finding the contact email:** check the site's About/Contact page, the author byline, or footer. If none, the generic `hello@` / `editor@` / `info@` usually works for roundups.

## PART 2 — The pitch templates

> Personalize the **first line** of every email (mention their specific article). Generic = ignored. Keep it short. One follow-up after 5–7 days, then move on.

### Template 1 — Roundup add (for "Upwork alternatives" articles)
**Subject:** Quick addition for your Upwork alternatives piece?

> Hi [Name],
>
> Just read your roundup of Upwork alternatives — [one specific, genuine detail, e.g. "the point about Fiverr's hidden revision costs was spot on"].
>
> Would you consider adding Hyrde? It's an AI-native alternative built around the exact complaints your readers have: every freelancer is pre-vetted before clients see them, matches come back in ~60 seconds, and the fee is a flat 8% instead of the 22–34% effective take rate people hit on the big platforms.
>
> Comparison here if useful: https://hyrde.net/compare
>
> Either way, thanks for the genuinely helpful article.
>
> [Your name], founder, Hyrde

### Template 2 — Data / Rate Index (for blogs that cite pricing)
**Subject:** Free 2026 freelance rate data for [Site]?

> Hi [Name],
>
> I saw your piece on [topic, e.g. "freelance developer pricing"] — [specific detail].
>
> We just published the Hyrde Freelance Rate Index: real 2026 hourly rates across 24 skills and 12 cities, with junior/mid/senior ranges and a transparent methodology. It's free to cite, no signup wall.
>
> https://hyrde.net/rates
>
> If any of it's useful for your readers, a link back is all we ask. Happy to pull custom cuts of the data if you ever need a specific stat.
>
> [Your name], Hyrde

### Template 3 — Founder story / cold-but-warm (for newsletters, smaller blogs)
**Subject:** Why I built an Upwork alternative after watching clients get burned

> Hi [Name],
>
> Big fan of [their newsletter/blog] — [specific detail].
>
> I built Hyrde after seeing the same story over and over: clients losing thousands to disappearing contractors, fake "Top Rated" badges, and reviews that get scrubbed. We vet talent before clients ever see them, AI-match in 60 seconds, and charge a flat 8%.
>
> If that's an angle your audience would care about, I'd love to share more — happy to write a guest post or just be a source. No pressure either way.
>
> [Your name], founder, Hyrde

### Follow-up (send once, 5–7 days later, reply to your own email)
> Hi [Name] — just floating this back to the top of your inbox in case it got buried. Totally understand if it's not a fit. Thanks either way!

## Rules of the road
- **Never buy links** — Google penalizes paid link schemes. Earn them.
- **Personalize line 1, always.** It's the difference between 0% and ~10% reply rates.
- **One follow-up max.** Persistence past that just burns goodwill.
- **Lead with value** (the free data / a genuinely better option for *their readers*), not "please link to me."
- Don't mass-BCC. Send individually.
